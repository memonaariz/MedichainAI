## 🏥 **Diabetes Management - Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus**

### **First-Line Treatment: Metformin Hydrochloride**

#### **Medication Details**
- **Generic Name**: Metformin Hydrochloride
- **Medication Class**: Biguanide
- **Mechanism of Action**: Reduces hepatic glucose production and increases insulin sensitivity
- **FDA Approval**: Fully approved for Type 2 diabetes management

#### **Dosage Instructions**
- **Starting Dose**: 500mg twice daily with meals
- **Titration Schedule**: Increase by 500mg every 1-2 weeks based on tolerance
- **Target Dose**: 1000mg twice daily (2000mg total daily)
- **Maximum Dose**: 2550mg daily (850mg three times daily)

#### **Administration Guidelines**
- **Timing**: Take with breakfast and dinner
- **Food Requirement**: Must be taken with food to minimize side effects
- **Consistency**: Take at the same times daily
- **Missed Dose**: Take as soon as remembered, skip if close to next dose

#### **Monitoring Requirements**
- **Daily**: Blood glucose monitoring (2-4 times initially)
- **Weekly**: Weight measurement, blood pressure
- **Monthly**: HbA1c testing (if not done quarterly)
- **Quarterly**: HbA1c, kidney function, comprehensive foot examination
- **Annually**: Eye examination, dental examination, vitamin B12

#### **Safety Considerations**
- **Contraindications**: Severe kidney disease (eGFR < 30), metabolic acidosis
- **Side Effects**: Nausea, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort (usually transient)
- **Monitoring**: Watch for signs of lactic acidosis
- **Precautions**: Avoid excessive alcohol consumption

### **Alternative Treatment: Sulfonylurea (Glimepiride)**

#### **When to Use**
- Metformin contraindicated or not tolerated
- Need for additional glucose control
- Patient preference for oral medication

#### **Dosage Instructions**
- **Starting Dose**: 1mg once daily
- **Target Dose**: 4-8mg once daily
- **Administration**: 30 minutes before breakfast
- **Monitoring**: Blood glucose for hypoglycemia

## 🩺 **Hypertension Management - Essential Hypertension**

### **First-Line Treatment: ACE Inhibitor (Lisinopril)**

#### **Medication Details**
- **Generic Name**: Lisinopril
- **Medication Class**: Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme (ACE) Inhibitor
- **Mechanism of Action**: Inhibits ACE, reducing blood pressure
- **FDA Approval**: Fully approved for hypertension treatment

#### **Dosage Instructions**
- **Starting Dose**: 10mg once daily
- **Titration Schedule**: Increase by 10mg every 2-4 weeks based on response
- **Target Dose**: 20-40mg once daily
- **Maximum Dose**: 80mg daily (rarely needed)

#### **Administration Guidelines**
- **Timing**: Take at the same time daily, preferably in the morning
- **Food**: Can be taken with or without food
- **Consistency**: Maintain regular dosing schedule
- **Missed Dose**: Take as soon as remembered, skip if close to next dose

#### **Monitoring Requirements**
- **Daily**: Blood pressure measurement (morning and evening)
- **Weekly**: Blood pressure trend analysis, side effect monitoring
- **Monthly**: Kidney function assessment, electrolyte monitoring
- **Quarterly**: Comprehensive blood pressure evaluation

#### **Safety Considerations**
- **Contraindications**: Pregnancy, history of angioedema, bilateral renal artery stenosis
- **Side Effects**: Dry cough (10-15% of patients), dizziness, hyperkalemia
- **Monitoring**: Watch for angioedema (swelling of face, lips, tongue)
- **Precautions**: Avoid pregnancy, monitor kidney function

### **Alternative Treatment: Calcium Channel Blocker (Amlodipine)**

#### **When to Use**
- ACE inhibitor contraindicated
- Need for additional blood pressure control
- African American patients (better response)

#### **Dosage Instructions**
- **Starting Dose**: 5mg once daily
- **Target Dose**: 5-10mg once daily
- **Administration**: Take at the same time daily
- **Monitoring**: Blood pressure, ankle edema assessment

## ❤️ **Heart Disease Prevention - Statin Therapy**

### **Primary Prevention: Atorvastatin Calcium**

#### **Medication Details**
- **Generic Name**: Atorvastatin Calcium
- **Medication Class**: HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitor (Statin)
- **Mechanism of Action**: Inhibits cholesterol synthesis in the liver
- **FDA Approval**: Fully approved for cardiovascular risk reduction

#### **Dosage Instructions**
- **Starting Dose**: 10-20mg once daily at bedtime
- **Target Dose**: 20-80mg once daily based on cardiovascular risk
- **Administration**: Take at bedtime for optimal effect
- **Consistency**: Take at the same time daily

#### **Monitoring Requirements**
- **Initial**: Liver function tests every 3 months
- **Ongoing**: Liver function tests every 6 months
- **Lipid Profile**: Every 6 months
- **Creatine Kinase**: If muscle symptoms develop
- **Blood Glucose**: Every 6 months

#### **Safety Considerations**
- **Contraindications**: Active liver disease, pregnancy, hypersensitivity
- **Side Effects**: Muscle pain, liver enzyme elevation, gastrointestinal symptoms
- **Monitoring**: Watch for muscle symptoms, liver function changes
- **Precautions**: Avoid grapefruit juice consumption

## 📊 **Personalized Dosage Adjustments**

### **Age-Based Adjustments**
- **Elderly (>65 years)**: Start with 50% of standard dose
- **Adults (18-65 years)**: Standard dosing
- **Pediatric (<18 years)**: Weight-based dosing

### **Kidney Function Adjustments**
- **Normal (eGFR >80)**: No change needed
- **Mild Impairment (eGFR 50-80)**: No change needed
- **Moderate Impairment (eGFR 30-50)**: Reduce dose by 25-50%
- **Severe Impairment (eGFR 15-30)**: Reduce dose by 50-75%
- **End-Stage (eGFR <15)**: Avoid or use with extreme caution

### **Weight-Based Adjustments**
- **Underweight (BMI <18.5)**: Consider reduced dosing
- **Normal Weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)**: Standard dosing
- **Overweight (BMI 25-29.9)**: Standard dosing
- **Obese (BMI >30)**: May require higher doses

## ⚠️ **Drug Interaction Database**

### **Metformin Interactions**
- **Alcohol**: Increased risk of lactic acidosis
- **Diuretics**: May increase kidney risk
- **Recommendation**: Limit alcohol, monitor kidney function

### **Lisinopril Interactions**
- **NSAIDs**: May reduce blood pressure effect
- **Lithium**: Increased lithium levels and toxicity risk
- **Recommendation**: Monitor blood pressure and kidney function

### **Atorvastatin Interactions**
- **Grapefruit Juice**: Increases statin levels and side effects
- **Warfarin**: May increase bleeding risk
- **Recommendation**: Avoid grapefruit juice, monitor INR

## 🚨 **Emergency Situations & Red Flags**

### **Diabetes Emergencies**
- **Severe Hypoglycemia**: Blood glucose <54 mg/dL
- **Diabetic Ketoacidosis**: Blood glucose >250 mg/dL with ketones
- **Hyperosmolar State**: Blood glucose >600 mg/dL
- **Action**: Seek immediate medical attention

### **Hypertension Emergencies**
- **Hypertensive Crisis**: Blood pressure >180/120 mmHg
- **Organ Damage**: Chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache
- **Action**: Emergency room evaluation required

### **Statin Emergencies**
- **Severe Muscle Pain**: Unexplained muscle weakness or pain
- **Liver Symptoms**: Yellowing of skin, dark urine, abdominal pain
- **Action**: Stop medication, contact healthcare provider immediately
